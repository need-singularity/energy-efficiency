We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"On the uniqueness of sigma(n) - tau(n) = 8"** for consideration for publication in the International Journal of Number Theory.

**Summary of the result.** We prove [or: present strong computational evidence for the conjecture] that the first perfect number n = 6 is the unique integer n >= 2 satisfying the equation sigma(n) - tau(n) = 8, where sigma(n) is the sum of divisors of n and tau(n) is the number of divisors of n. The proof exploits the multiplicativity of both arithmetic functions and proceeds by case analysis over the prime factorization of n.

**Context and significance.** This result complements the known identity sigma(n) * phi(n) = n * tau(n) iff n = 6 (for n >= 2), providing a second independent arithmetic characterization of the number 6. While the multiplicative identity sigma * phi = n * tau has received attention in the literature on arithmetic functions, the additive equation sigma - tau = c appears not to have been studied systematically for small values of c. Our result addresses the case c = 8 and reveals a connection to the Binary Golay code [24, 12, 8], whose parameters [length, dimension, minimum distance] coincide with [sigma(6) * phi(6), sigma(6), sigma(6) - tau(6)].

**Novelty.** To our knowledge, the uniqueness of n = 6 as a solution to sigma(n) - tau(n) = 8 has not appeared previously in the literature. The connection between this arithmetic identity and error-correcting code parameters appears to be new.

**Suitability for IJNT.** The manuscript falls squarely within the scope of IJNT, addressing questions in elementary and analytic number theory (MSC 11A25, 11A05, 11N64). The techniques -- multiplicative function analysis, case exhaustion over prime factorizations, and computational verification -- are standard tools in the study of arithmetic functions.
