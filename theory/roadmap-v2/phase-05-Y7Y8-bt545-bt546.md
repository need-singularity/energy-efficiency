### 3.2 Lemma 1 Rigorous Draft Development (unconditional)

#### 3.2.1 Lemma 1 Statement

**Lemma 1 (CRT decomposition, unconditional)**: for all elliptic curves $E/\mathbb{Q}$ and coprime $\gcd(m, n) = 1$ positive integers $m, n$,

$$
|\mathrm{Sel}_{mn}(E)| = |\mathrm{Sel}_m(E)| \cdot |\mathrm{Sel}_n(E)|.
$$

#### 3.2.2 Draft Development

**Step 1** — CRT at Galois module level.

The structure of $E[N] = \{P \in E(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}) : N \cdot P = O\}$ is $E[N] \cong (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^2$ (Galois module). For coprime $m, n$, Chinese remainder theorem gives at abelian group level

$$
E[mn] \cong E[m] \oplus E[n]
$$

Galois-equivariantly. Specifically, if $P \in E[mn]$, take $am + bn = 1$ using Bezout and decompose

$$
P = (an)\cdot P + (bm) \cdot P \in E[m] + E[n]
$$

(first term: $n(an)P = a(mn)P = 0$, so $(an)P \in E[m]$; second similarly). This decomposition commutes with Galois action.

**Step 2** — Direct sum decomposition of Galois cohomology.

Since $E[mn] \cong E[m] \oplus E[n]$ holds as Galois modules,

$$
H^1(G_\mathbb{Q}, E[mn]) \cong H^1(G_\mathbb{Q}, E[m]) \oplus H^1(G_\mathbb{Q}, E[n]).
$$

Same at local gauge:

$$
H^1(G_{\mathbb{Q}_v}, E[mn]) \cong H^1(G_{\mathbb{Q}_v}, E[m]) \oplus H^1(G_{\mathbb{Q}_v}, E[n]).
$$

**Step 3** — Kummer map compatibility.

$E(\mathbb{Q})/mnE(\mathbb{Q}) \hookrightarrow H^1(G_\mathbb{Q}, E[mn])$ Kummer map also decomposes in CRT:

$$
E(\mathbb{Q})/mnE(\mathbb{Q}) \cong E(\mathbb{Q})/mE(\mathbb{Q}) \oplus E(\mathbb{Q})/nE(\mathbb{Q}).
$$

This is application of the fact that $mn$-quotient of finitely generated abelian group $E(\mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}^r \oplus E(\mathbb{Q})_{\mathrm{tors}}$ naturally decomposes at coprime $m, n$.

**Step 4** — Selmer condition definition.

$\mathrm{Sel}_N(E)$ is the set of elements of

$$
\mathrm{Sel}_N(E) = \ker\left( H^1(G_\mathbb{Q}, E[N]) \to \prod_v H^1(G_{\mathbb{Q}_v}, E) \right)
$$

that lie within the local image of $E(\mathbb{Q}_v)/N E(\mathbb{Q}_v)$ at each $v$.

**Step 5** — Selmer set splits as direct sum.

The decompositions of Step 2, 3 are Galois-equivariant on both global/local sides and compatible with Kummer map. Local condition at each $v$ is also compatible with $E[mn]$ decomposition. Therefore

$$
\mathrm{Sel}_{mn}(E) \cong \mathrm{Sel}_m(E) \oplus \mathrm{Sel}_n(E)
$$

group isomorphism. Since finite group, taking sizes:

$$
|\mathrm{Sel}_{mn}(E)| = |\mathrm{Sel}_m(E)| \cdot |\mathrm{Sel}_n(E)|. \qquad \blacksquare
$$

### 3.4 Sel_6 Conditional Theorem

#### 3.4.2 Theorem 1 Draft Path
